Note on Chlorine

Symbol: Cl

Atomic Number: 17

Group: Halogens (Group 17)

Chlorine is a greenish-yellow gas at room temperature with a pungent odor. It is highly reactive and toxic, commonly used in disinfectants and bleaching agents.

Chlorine exists in nature mainly as chloride salts (e.g., sodium chloride - table salt). It is essential for water purification and is widely used in the production of plastics such as PVC.

In reactions, chlorine acts as an oxidizing agent. It readily forms compounds with many elements, particularly with metals to form chlorides.

Chlorine is harmful if inhaled and must be handled with care.
