Physics Note: Friction

1. What is Friction?

Friction is a force that opposes the relative motion or tendency of such motion of two surfaces in contact.

2. Types of Friction

  • Static Friction: Acts when there is no motion between the surfaces.
  • Kinetic (Sliding) Friction: Acts when objects slide against each other.
  • Rolling Friction: Acts when an object rolls over a surface.
  • Fluid Friction: Resistance offered by fluids (liquids and gases).

3. Laws of Friction

  • Friction is proportional to the normal force.
  • It is independent of the contact area.
  • It depends on the nature of the surfaces in contact.

4. Advantages of Friction

  • Enables walking
  • Allows vehicles to move
  • Helps hold and grip objects

5. Disadvantages of Friction

  • Causes wear and tear
  • Leads to energy loss
  • Generates heat in machines

6. Reducing Friction

  • Use of lubricants
  • Using wheels or ball bearings
  • Smoothing surfaces
