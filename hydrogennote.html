Chemistry Note: Hydrogen

Introduction

Hydrogen is the first element in the periodic table and the lightest known element. It has the atomic number 1 and is represented by the symbol H.

Properties of Hydrogen

  • Atomic number: 1
  • Atomic mass: 1.008 u
  • Colorless, odorless, tasteless gas
  • Highly flammable
  • Exists as a diatomic molecule (H₂)

Occurrence

Hydrogen is the most abundant element in the universe. It is found in water (H₂O), organic compounds, and in stars including the sun.

Uses of Hydrogen

  • Used in the production of ammonia (Haber process)
  • Used as rocket fuel
  • Used in hydrogenation of oils
  • Potential future fuel in hydrogen fuel cells

Isotopes of Hydrogen

  • Protium (¹H)
  • Deuterium (²H or D)
  • Tritium (³H or T)

Conclusion

Hydrogen is an important element in chemistry and energy. It plays a key role in chemical reactions and has growing significance in clean energy solutions.
