Sulphur - Chemistry Note

Introduction

Sulphur is a non-metallic chemical element with the symbol S and atomic number 16. It is commonly found in nature and plays a vital role in biological and industrial processes.

Properties of Sulphur

  • Yellow in color
  • Brittle solid at room temperature
  • Non-metallic and poor conductor of electricity
  • Insoluble in water

Allotropes of Sulphur

Sulphur exists mainly in two allotropes:

    
  • Rhombic Sulphur – stable at room temperature
  • Monoclinic Sulphur – stable at higher temperature

Uses of Sulphur

  • Manufacture of sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄)
  • Vulcanization of rubber
  • Used in fungicides and insecticides
  • In the production of matches and fireworks

Occurrence

Sulphur is found in volcanic regions, in minerals like pyrite and galena, and as a part of proteins in living organisms.

Compounds of Sulphur

  • Hydrogen sulphide (H₂S)
  • Sulphur dioxide (SO₂)
  • Sulphur trioxide (SO₃)
