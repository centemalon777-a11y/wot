Chemistry Note: Organic Chemistry

Introduction

Organic chemistry is the branch of chemistry that deals with the study of compounds containing carbon. These compounds are known as organic compounds.

Characteristics of Organic Compounds

  • Contain carbon and hydrogen (may also contain O, N, S, etc.)
  • Mostly covalent compounds
  • Have low melting and boiling points
  • Usually insoluble in water
  • Combustible in nature

Classification of Organic Compounds

  • Alkanes – single bonds (saturated hydrocarbons)
  • Alkenes – contain at least one double bond
  • Alkynes – contain at least one triple bond
  • Aromatic compounds – contain benzene ring

Functional Groups

Functional groups are specific groups of atoms within molecules that are responsible for the characteristic chemical reactions of those molecules.

  • -OH (Hydroxyl) → Alcohols
  • -COOH (Carboxyl) → Carboxylic acids
  • -CHO (Aldehyde)
  • -NH₂ (Amino) → Amines

Importance of Organic Chemistry

  • Used in the production of drugs and medicines
  • Important in agriculture (fertilizers, pesticides)
  • Used in fuels (petrol, diesel, gas)
  • Key in polymers and plastics manufacturing

Conclusion

Organic chemistry plays a vital role in modern life, influencing industries such as medicine, agriculture, and energy. Understanding its basic concepts is essential for every chemistry student.
