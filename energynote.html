Physics Note: Work, Energy, and Power

1. Work

Definition: Work is done when a force moves an object through a distance.

Formula: Work (W) = Force × Distance × cos(θ)

Unit: Joule (J)

Types of Work:

  • Positive Work
  • Negative Work
  • Zero Work

2. Energy

Definition: Energy is the capacity to do work.

Kinetic Energy (KE): KE = ½ mv²

Potential Energy (PE): PE = mgh

Unit: Joule (J)

3. Power

Definition: Power is the rate at which work is done.

Formula: Power = Work / Time

Unit: Watt (W), 1 hp ≈ 746 W

4. Efficiency

Efficiency (%) = (Useful energy output / Total input) × 100

5. Law of Conservation of Energy

Energy cannot be created or destroyed; only converted from one form to another.
