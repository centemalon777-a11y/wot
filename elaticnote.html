Physics Note: Elasticity

1. What is Elasticity?

Elasticity is the ability of a material to return to its original shape and size after the external force causing deformation is removed.

2. Hooke's Law

Hooke's Law states that the extension of an elastic material is directly proportional to the applied force, provided the elastic limit is not exceeded.

Formula: F = kx

  • F = force applied
  • k = spring constant
  • x = extension

3. Elastic Limit

This is the maximum amount of stress a material can withstand without permanent deformation.

4. Young’s Modulus

Young’s modulus (E) is the ratio of stress to strain within the elastic limit.

Formula: E = Stress / Strain

5. Applications of Elasticity

  • Design of springs
  • Shock absorbers
  • Construction of bridges and buildings
